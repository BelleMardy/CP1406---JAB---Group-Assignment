Testing Details

    Testing was carried out using several people and it was found that the website functioned adequatley in the areas of learnability, efficiency, memorability, errors and satisfaction. Task oriented tests were also carried out such as buying tickets, becoming a member, emailing people within the Barrier Reef Orchestra (BRO), learning who the artist was for the next concert and joining the mailing list. Feedback given from testing resulted in improvements being made to the website which made it more intuitive, while improving the asthetics and usability of site.

    There are potential issuse with the search function as we understand it needs to be a functioning website prior to operating correctly ie; doing a search within the website. Most of the pages on the website are responsive, however there are still some unresolved issues in relation to a fully responsive website.

    References - All code was created by JAB. All images and content were obtained from either the existing Barrier Reef Orchrestra website or JCU supplied information. The exception is the feeder page, where information was obtained from an ABC News Article.

PMI

    Pluses: We worked well together as a team by having regular meetings, collaberation on ideas, concepts and taking on tasks to be completed and completing those tasks. We also supported other team members as required.

    Minuses: A negative aspect was competing priorities of other subjects which had assignments and exams due at the same time as this assignment. There were issues around the version control in GitHub. There was a significant amount of confusion by incorrect information being supplied or unavailable at given times.

    Interesting: The most interesting aspect was the different thought processes, understanding of assignment requirements and successes in completing allocated tasks. As a team we created a plan, however during the development and testing stages we realised that those plans needed to be adjusted to suit the desired final outcome.
